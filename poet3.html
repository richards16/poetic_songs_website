HALLELUJAH

SUMMARY

"Hallelujah" explores themes of love, spirituality, and human frailty through a series of biblical references and contemporary reflections. The song's verses often present contradictory emotions and images, blending the sacred with the profane, the romantic with the spiritual.

POEM

Oh, love, you are missing today
And, suddenly, everyone knows your name.
Oh, love, do you remember the way
You watched the seasons shift and change?

You're not here today, love
You'll visit and revisit
Yet you refuse to stay
And that's not enough, is it?

"Sweetheart, what have you done to us?"
We sang those words until we couldn't breathe
"Don't lie, I know we're fixing to die,"
And you were first to leave.

"Sweetheart, what have you done to us?"
We sang those words until we couldn't breathe
"Don't lie, I know we're fixing to die,"
And you were first to leave.

-Leonard Cohen

ABOUT AN AUTHOR

Leonard Cohen (1934-2016) was a Canadian singer-songwriter, poet, and novelist, widely regarded as one of the most influential artists of his generation. Born in Montreal, Canada, Cohen initially pursued a career as a poet and novelist before transitioning to music in the late 1960s.
